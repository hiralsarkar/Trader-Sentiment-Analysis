Key Takeaways

🟢 Extreme Greed is the strongest regime: highest win rate (46.5%) and highest avg PnL/trade ($67.9), with smaller position sizes. Note: at the broader "Greed" bucket level this edge shrinks to $53.9 vs $49.2 for Fear — the real outperformance is concentrated in the extreme euphoria tail, not greed in general.
🔴 Extreme Fear is the weakest regime: lowest win rate (37.1%) and lowest avg PnL/trade ($34.5), while plain Fear sees the largest average trade size (~$7.8k) — a risky, inverted sizing pattern.
📉 Raw sentiment score barely correlates with daily PnL (r ≈ -0.08) — the categorical regime (especially "Extreme" labels) matters far more than the 0–100 number.
👥 PnL is concentration-driven — the top 5 of 32 accounts generated $6.36M of the $10.25M total PnL (~62%), so strategies should be validated per-account, not just in aggregate.

🎯 Recommendations to Maximize KPIs

📈 Increase conviction in Extreme Greed — win rate (46.5%) and avg PnL/trade ($67.9) are regime highs while avg size is already smallest ($3.1k). Room to size up.
🛡️ Cut size 30-50% in Extreme Fear — lowest win rate (37.1%) and lowest avg PnL/trade ($34.5) of any regime; reduces drawdowns.
⚠️ Fix the inverted sizing in plain Fear — largest avg trade size ($7.8k) paired with only a middling 42.1% win rate is the opposite of optimal risk allocation.
🪙 Build per-coin, per-regime playbooks — e.g. TRUMP: +$81 avg PnL in Fear vs -$454 in Greed; FARTCOIN: -$98 in Fear vs +$19 in Greed. Same coin, opposite signal by regime.
💰 Take partial profits into Greed — sell share already rises to 52.9% in Greed vs ~50% elsewhere; formalize this as a scaled-exit rule.
🔍 Audit bottom-5 accounts — they are consistent PnL drags while the top 5 drive ~62% of profit; reduce their allocation or retrain their approach.
💸 Reduce fee drag in Fear — avg fee per trade is highest here ($1.50 vs $0.68 in Extreme Greed), likely from over-trading; fewer, higher-conviction entries would help.
